# Solid-State Battery Research - Q1 2026

## Section 1: Executive Summary

As of February 2026, our internal research at **QuantumVolt Labs** has achieved a breakthrough in solid-state electrolyte stability. Unlike current lithium-ion batteries that use liquid electrolytes, our **QV-1 Prototype** utilizes a proprietary sulfide-based solid electrolyte.

## Section 2: The QV-1 Prototype Specifications

- **Energy Density:** 450 Wh/kg.
- **Operating Temperature:** -30°C to 80°C.
- **Cycle Life:** 5,000 cycles with 90% capacity retention.
- **Internal Project Code:** "Silver-Bullet".

## Section 3: Current Limitations (Internal Only)

While the QV-1 outperforms market leaders, we have identified three critical internal limitations:

1. **Interface Resistance:** Significant resistance still occurs at the cathode-electrolyte boundary after 1,000 rapid-charge cycles.
2. **Manufacturing Scalability:** The "Silver-Bullet" electrolyte requires a vacuum-sealed environment for assembly, increasing production costs by 40% compared to traditional cells.
3. **Dendrite Growth:** High-pressure environments (above 5MPa) are still required to prevent lithium dendrites from penetrating the sulfide layer.
